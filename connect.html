Good questions

Things people usually ask.

Who does Dancing Village work with?+

Nonprofits, media companies, cultural institutions, mission-led businesses, and purpose-driven founders — any organization with a meaningful initiative that needs corporate partners and funding behind it. What matters more than sector or size is whether the mission is real and the leadership is ready to commit to the work.

What does an engagement typically look like?+

Every engagement is different, because every organization's needs are different. Some clients need a strategy sprint of a few weeks; others need an ongoing partner for a year or more. We always start with a conversation to figure out what fits your situation — and I come back with a concrete scope and fee before anything begins.

Do you work on commission or retainer?+

Primarily on project fees or retainer, depending on the scope and nature of the work. I don't take commissions on deals I close — I find that structure creates the wrong incentives and can compromise the relationships I'm building on your behalf. Transparency around compensation is important to me.

Can you work with small organizations?+

Yes — organizational size is less important than mission clarity and readiness. Some of the most impactful partnerships I've helped build were for smaller organizations with very focused missions. That said, I do need you to be serious about the investment of time, trust, and resources that this kind of work requires.

What if I'm not sure I'm ready?+

Reach out anyway. Part of what we do in a first conversation is figure out whether the timing is right. Sometimes organizations aren't quite ready to go to market — and the most valuable thing I can do is help you understand what needs to happen first. That's a useful conversation regardless of whether we work together.
