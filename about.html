About Tammy

Spent 25 years
building the work.
Now I bring it to you.

"She is skilled at working seamlessly across matrixed teams. She identifies synergies and a shared sense of purpose, and executes with utmost professionalism, tenacity, and a solutions-oriented mindset." Sophie Ross — Communications Consultant & Executive Coach

Tammy Bordeaux has spent her career at the intersection of mission and commerce — building the partnerships that fund, amplify, and advance the work of organizations that matter.

Dancing Village Partners is the practice she built to bring 25 years of that experience directly to the leaders who need it. It's collaborative by design: Tammy is the constant, and she assembles the right team around each project.

01

The beginning

Building commercial partnerships in media.

Before she built partnership programs at the world's most recognizable mission-driven organizations, Tammy spent years in commercial partnership roles at media companies including Rodale and Time Inc — learning how brands and publishers find each other, and what makes a sponsorship or partnership actually hold.

That foundation in media sales is what made everything that followed possible. She understood what corporate partners needed before she ever sat across from them on behalf of a mission.

02

National Geographic · 13 Years

Where it became the real thing.

Tammy spent 13 years at National Geographic — growing from Director to Vice President of Corporate Partnerships. Her job was to build and run the partnerships program: finding the right companies, developing the relationships, and bringing in the revenue that let the mission run.

She worked with brands, foundations, media partners, and cultural institutions across dozens of major initiatives. What those 13 years gave her was rare: a deep understanding of how corporate partners think, what they need to say yes, and how to build something both sides are proud of long after the ink is dry.

13 years. Hundreds of partnerships. One mission: find the right companies and get them to yes.
03

The early years · Rodale · Time Inc · Tribeca

Breadth across media and culture.

Before and after Nat Geo, Tammy built her experience across the media and cultural landscape. Her early work at Rodale and Time Inc gave her the commercial partnerships foundation. Her later role as SVP of Partnerships at Tribeca Festival brought that expertise into one of the world's most complex and high-profile cultural partnership environments — where film, media, technology, and social impact converge.

04

The founder chapter

I didn't just build partnerships. I also needed them.

While building her corporate career, Tammy co-founded Rivertowns Village Green, a 501(c)(3), and created The Tarrytown and Sleepy Hollow Farmers Market — recently voted the #1 farmers market in New York State.

This wasn't a side project. It was a full nonprofit operation: building a board, navigating community politics, designing programming, finding corporate sponsors, writing grants, and rallying community support — all while serving real people with food assistance, education, and cultural events.

Running an organization that needed partners and funding — while also spending decades finding partners and funding for others — gave her a perspective that shapes everything about how she works with clients.

05

Why Dancing Village

In 2023, she decided to spend all her time on this.

After 25 years, Tammy started Dancing Village Partners to bring everything she'd built — the experience, the relationships, the instincts, the joy of the work — directly to the organizations that need it most.

The name matters. A dancing village isn't a solo act. It's people moving together, toward something bigger than any one of them. That's what a great partnership feels like — and it's how Dancing Village itself operates. Tammy is the constant; she builds the team around each engagement, bringing in the right specialists, strategists, and collaborators depending on what the project calls for.

Today she works with nonprofits, media companies, cultural institutions, and mission-led organizations — helping them find, build, and close the partnerships that fund their next chapter.

How I show up

The things I believe about this work.

01

Relationships over transactions

The best partnerships are built on genuine shared interest. I spend time understanding both parties before a single pitch is made.

02

The right partner beats the big partner

A perfect-fit mid-size company is worth more than a misaligned Fortune 500. I'm not chasing logos — I'm building relationships that hold.

03

Teams over lone wolves

Every engagement is collaborative. I bring in the right specialists — not because I can't do it alone, but because the work is better with the right people around it.

04

Honest before optimistic

I'll tell you when a strategy isn't working and when a prospect isn't the right fit. My job is to get you to a yes — not to tell you everything is going great.
